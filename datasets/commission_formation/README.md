# EUR_DATA: Commission Formation Dataset (2024-2029)

Structured dataset on the 2024-2029 European Commission formation process and work programme negotiations. Deliverable D1.2 / Milestone MS3.

## Quick Start

```bash
# Install dependencies
pip install -r requirements.txt

# Run the full pipeline
python run_pipeline.py

# Run a single step
python run_pipeline.py --step 5    # investiture vote only

# Skip PDF downloads (if already cached)
python run_pipeline.py --skip-download

# Run tests
pytest tests/ -v
```

## Dataset at a Glance

Six interlinked tables covering the full arc of Commission formation, from the June 2024 European Parliament elections through the adoption of the 2025 Commission Work Programme in February 2025.

| Table | Rows | What it captures |
|-------|------|------------------|
| `commissioners.csv` | 27 | The College: one row per member state, with portfolio, party, gender, and DG assignments |
| `mission_letter_commitments.csv` | 1057 | Policy commitments extracted from 26 presidential mission letters |
| `hearings.csv` | 26 | Confirmation hearing dates, responsible committees, outcomes, and EP press-release sources |
| `investiture_vote.csv` | 688 | MEP-level roll-call votes on the full College (27 Nov 2024) |
| `work_programme_items.csv` | 130 | Legislative and policy items from the Commission Work Programme 2025 |
| `formation_timeline.csv` | 13 | Key institutional milestones from elections to CWP adoption |

All tables are linked through `commissioner_id` (a 3-letter key per commissioner). The vote table uses `ep_party_group` as a shared dimension with the commissioner table.

## What's in the Data

### The College of Commissioners

The 27-member College reflects the political balance of the 2024-2029 Parliament:

- **Party composition**: EPP dominates with 14 commissioners (52%), followed by S&D and Renew with 5 each, plus 1 ECR (Fitto, Italy), 1 PfE (Varhelyi, Hungary), and 1 independent (Kadis, Cyprus, a non-partisan technocrat coded `Independent`/`Independent`).
- **Gender split**: 11 women (41%) and 16 men (59%).
- **Role hierarchy**: 1 President (von der Leyen), 5 Executive Vice-Presidents, 1 High Representative/Vice-President (Kallas), and 20 Commissioners.
- **Coverage**: All 27 EU member states, one commissioner per country.

Each row includes the commissioner's portfolio title, responsible DGs, EP party group, national party, mission letter URL, and hearing date.

**Coding notes:**

- Party affiliation is coded **as at nomination**. Sefcovic is coded S&D although his national party (Smer) has been suspended from the PES since October 2023; this is a deliberate coding decision.
- **EVP counting convention**: this dataset codes 5 Executive Vice-Presidents and records the High Representative/Vice-President (Kallas) as a separate role. The Commission's own communications count six EVPs including Kallas; under that convention women hold 4 of 6 EVP posts (under the dataset convention: 3 of 5).

### The Investiture Vote

On 27 November 2024, the European Parliament voted to approve the full College: **370 for, 282 against, 36 abstentions** (688 MEPs participating).

The vote data reveals the coalition structure underlying the Commission's mandate:

| Party group | For | Against | Abstain | Total |
|-------------|-----|---------|---------|-------|
| PPE | 151 | 25 | 2 | 178 |
| S&D | 90 | 25 | 18 | 133 |
| PfE | 0 | 84 | 0 | 84 |
| ECR | 33 | 39 | 4 | 76 |
| Renew | 67 | 0 | 6 | 73 |
| Verts/ALE | 27 | 19 | 6 | 52 |
| The Left | 0 | 43 | 0 | 43 |
| NI | 2 | 24 | 0 | 26 |
| ESN | 0 | 23 | 0 | 23 |

The supporting coalition (PPE + S&D + Renew) provided the bulk of "for" votes, while PfE, The Left, and ESN voted unanimously against. ECR was divided: Italy's 24 Fratelli d'Italia MEPs voted for (reflecting PM Meloni's support for EVP Fitto), while Poland's 20 PiS MEPs voted against.

Notable national patterns:
- **France** voted 61-18 against, the most lopsided national delegation, driven by PfE (Rassemblement National), S&D, and The Left opposition.
- **Italy** voted 52-23 in favour, with ECR (Fratelli d'Italia) support proving pivotal.
- **Spain** voted 37-21 against, unusually including all 21 Spanish PPE members (Partido Popular) breaking the group line.
- **Germany** (largest delegation, 92 votes cast) voted 51-28-13, with S&D members abstaining rather than voting for.

Country enrichment covers 100% of MEPs (688 of 688). The `national_party` field is not populated in the current pipeline version.

### Mission Letter Commitments

1057 specific policy commitments extracted from the 26 mission letters issued by President von der Leyen to each commissioner-designate (von der Leyen herself, as President, has no mission letter).

- **Extraction confidence**: 440 high-confidence commitments (extracted from bullet-point lists) and 617 medium-confidence (from directive sentences and legislative references).
- **Commitment types**: coordination (312), policy (108), report (33), review (51), legislative (63), other (490). Types are assigned by an LLM classifier (Claude Sonnet 4.6) that reads each commitment in full, distinguishing e.g. law-making from enforcement; the `classification_method` column records whether the type came from the LLM (`llm`) or the keyword fallback (`keyword`). See the codebook.
- **Top commissioners by commitment count**: Sefcovic (59), Virkkunen (49), Brunner (49), Sejourne (47), Minzatu (47). The fewest: Kos (31).
- Average: ~41 commitments per commissioner.

**Boilerplate**: a large share of the commitments is template text repeated across letters. Every mission letter closes with a near-identical block of roughly 20 shared commitments (working methods, coordination duties, reporting obligations). Two columns make this measurable: `n_letters_sharing_text` (how many of the 26 letters contain the exact commitment text) and `is_boilerplate` (true where the text is shared by more than 13 of the 26 letters). 515 of the 1057 rows (49%) are boilerplate, covering just 20 distinct template texts; the dataset contains 562 distinct commitment texts overall. The boilerplate includes 26 of the 63 legislative, 26 of the 33 report, and 26 of the 51 review commitments (one template text each), so raw type counts overstate portfolio-specific pledges. Filter on `is_boilerplate == False` for portfolio-specific analysis.

Each commitment includes the full text, a short summary (guaranteed <=100 characters including the ellipsis), the section heading from the letter, and the surrounding paragraph for context.

### Hearings

26 confirmation hearings held over 4-12 November 2024. The schedule is verified row-by-row against the EP's adopted hearings calendar and the individual EP press releases; each row carries the press-release link in `source_url`.

- **Main block**: 4-7 November (20 hearings: 4 on 4 Nov, 6 on 5 Nov, 6 on 6 Nov, 4 on 7 Nov). No hearings took place on 8-11 November.
- **EVP day**: all six Executive Vice-President-level nominees (Fitto, Kallas, Minzatu, Sejourne, Ribera, Virkkunen; Kallas as High Representative/VP) were heard last, on 12 November (6 hearings).
- Joint-committee hearings list every lead committee in full (e.g. McGrath LIBE/IMCO/JURI, Ribera ENVI/ECON/ITRE), plus associated committees.
- All 26 nominees were eventually approved, but committee approvals are majority votes and several were contested and delayed: Ribera, Fitto, and Varhelyi required further written procedures and political negotiation before the EVP evaluations were concluded as a package on 20-21 November.

### Commission Work Programme 2025

130 items from the annexes of the CWP 2025 (COM(2025) 45), adopted 11 February 2025. The dataset covers four of the five official annexes:

| Annex | Items | Content |
|-------|-------|---------|
| I (New initiatives) | 52 | New policy and legislative initiatives |
| II (Evaluations and fitness checks) | 37 | Annual plan on evaluations and fitness checks |
| IV (Withdrawals) | 37 | Pending proposals to be withdrawn |
| V (Envisaged repeals) | 4 | Acts in force envisaged for repeal |

The official **Annex III (Pending proposals, 123 dossiers) is deliberately not extracted**: it is out of scope for this dataset, and pending files are tracked in the agenda_implementation dataset's term corpus.

- Annex I's 52 initiatives are unpacked from 45 numbered rows (a numbered entry can list several initiatives). By nature: 33 Non-legislative, 18 Legislative (matching the EPRS briefing count), and 1 "Non-legislative or legislative". One initiative ("An EU fit for enlargement: policy reviews and reforms") carries `indicative_timing` "tbd"; every other Annex I row has a quarter.
- **Withdrawals are not repeals.** Annex IV lists withdrawals of pending proposals that were never adopted, naming 22 regulations, 10 directives, 4 decisions, and 1 recommendation in the withdrawn proposals' titles; its `description` carries the COM references and the stated reasons for withdrawal. Annex V lists envisaged repeals of acts in force (3 regulations, 1 decision), with the stated reasons for repeal.

Annex I policy areas span competitiveness, security, decarbonisation, innovation, migration, and social fairness. Linking commitments thematically to the 52 new CWP initiatives in Annex I shows how mission letter priorities translate into the legislative agenda.

### Formation Timeline

13 institutional milestones spanning 250 days from the EP elections (6 June 2024) to the adoption of the CWP (11 February 2025):

1. EP elections (6-9 Jun 2024)
2. European Council nominates von der Leyen (27 Jun)
3. EP constitutive session / Metsola re-elected (16 Jul)
4. EP elects von der Leyen as President (18 Jul, 401 votes)
5. Commissioners-designate announced & mission letters published (17 Sep)
6. Confirmation hearings (4-12 Nov, closing with all six EVP-level nominees on 12 Nov)
7. Conference of Presidents concludes the hearings process and authorises publication of the evaluation letters (27 Nov, after the Conference of Committee Chairs on 26 Nov)
8. EP investiture vote (27 Nov, 370-282-36)
9. Commission takes office (1 Dec)
10. CWP 2025 adopted (11 Feb 2025)

## Cross-Table Relationships

```
commissioners (27)
├── commissioner_id ──→ mission_letter_commitments (1057)  [1:many]
├── commissioner_id ──→ hearings (26)                     [1:1, excl. President]
└── ep_party_group  ──→ investiture_vote (688)            [shared dimension]

work_programme_items (130)   ← standalone (linkable to commissioners via policy_area)
formation_timeline (13)      ← standalone (institutional chronology)
```

**Key joins:**
- `commissioners` + `investiture_vote` on `ep_party_group`: analyse how each commissioner's party group voted on the College.
- `commissioners` + `mission_letter_commitments` on `commissioner_id`: connect commitments to commissioner attributes (country, party, gender, portfolio).
- `commissioners` + `hearings` on `commissioner_id`: link hearing committees and dates to commissioner profiles.

## Working with the Data

```python
import pandas as pd

# Load core tables
comm = pd.read_csv("data/output/commissioners.csv")
votes = pd.read_csv("data/output/investiture_vote.csv")
commitments = pd.read_csv("data/output/mission_letter_commitments.csv")
wp = pd.read_csv("data/output/work_programme_items.csv")

# Party group vote breakdown
votes.groupby("ep_party_group")["vote"].value_counts().unstack(fill_value=0)

# High-confidence commitments only
high_conf = commitments[commitments["confidence"] == "high"]  # 440 rows
high_conf.groupby("commissioner_id").size().sort_values(ascending=False)

# Portfolio-specific pledges only (excluding the shared closing block)
specific = commitments[~commitments["is_boilerplate"]]

# Join commitments to commissioner metadata
merged = commitments.merge(
    comm[["commissioner_id", "country_name", "ep_party_group", "gender"]],
    on="commissioner_id"
)

# Commitments by party group
merged.groupby("ep_party_group").size()

# Gender split in commitment types
merged.groupby(["gender", "commitment_type"]).size().unstack(fill_value=0)

# Work programme by annex
wp["annex"].value_counts()
```

## Research Use Cases

### Coalition and Voting Behaviour

The investiture vote table enables analysis of the coalition dynamics behind the Commission's mandate. Party discipline can be measured by comparing within-group vote shares (e.g., PPE's 85% cohesion vs. ECR's split). National delegation behaviour reveals cross-cutting cleavages: Spain's PPE members breaking the group line, Italy's ECR backing the Commission, France's near-unanimous opposition across left and right.

### Gender and Representation

With 11 women among 27 commissioners, the data supports analysis of gendered portfolio assignment patterns. Joining commissioners to mission letter commitments allows comparison of commitment volumes and types by gender. The hearing data can reveal whether female commissioners were assigned to different committee configurations.

### Policy Priority Analysis

The 1057 mission letter commitments represent the President's policy expectations for each portfolio. Comparing commitment counts and types across commissioners reveals variation in workload and policy ambition (use `is_boilerplate` to strip the shared template block first). Linking commitments thematically to the 52 new CWP initiatives in Annex I shows how mission letter priorities translate into the legislative agenda.

### Institutional Process

The timeline and hearing data together capture the procedural rhythm of Commission formation: the 250-day span from elections to work programme, the scheduling of all six EVP-level hearings on the final day (12 November), and the contested committee evaluations (Ribera, Fitto, and Varhelyi required further written procedures before the EVP evaluations were concluded as a package on 20-21 November) are all observable in the data.

### Computational Text Analysis

The commitment texts (1057 rows of natural-language policy statements) and work programme titles/descriptions are suitable for NLP applications: topic modelling across portfolios, keyword extraction, semantic similarity between mission letter commitments and CWP items, or network analysis based on coordination mentions ("work closely with...") in the mission letters.

## Raw Data Availability

The public repository does not ship `data/raw/` (the 26 mission letter PDFs, the EP roll-call XML, and ~735 MEP API cache files). Rebuilding steps 1-6 re-downloads them from EC/EP sources, which rate-limit aggressively (HTTP 429) and whose URLs rot over time. The shipped LLM classification cache covers 100% of the 562 distinct commitment texts, so step 03 reproduces offline without an API key.

| Component | Estimated Accuracy | Notes |

**Data notes:**

- **Commitment boilerplate**: 49% of commitment rows are template text shared across letters (see Mission Letter Commitments above). Per-commissioner counts and type counts should be interpreted with `is_boilerplate` in mind.
- **CWP Annex III excluded**: the 123 pending-proposal dossiers in the official Annex III are deliberately out of scope; pending files are tracked in the agenda_implementation dataset.
- **Hearing outcomes**: `outcome = approved` conceals contested approvals; committee approvals are majority votes and several required further written procedures (see CODEBOOK).
- `investiture_vote.national_party` and the hearings `evaluation_letter_url` / `written_questions_url` / `video_url` columns are not populated in this release.
